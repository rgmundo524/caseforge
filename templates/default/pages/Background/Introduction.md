# Introduction
CipherBlade was retained by counsel for the affected party to conduct a blockchain forensic investigation concerning an alleged impersonation-based cryptocurrency investment fraud resulting in substantial financial loss. Applying specialized knowledge and reliable forensic methodologies, this report analyzes preserved transaction records and related evidence documenting the transfer of approximately 1,643.78 ETH (≈ $5.2 million USD) to a purported investment platform from which withdrawals were later restricted. The analyses are presented to assist the trier of fact in understanding the factual context, flow of funds, and investigative implications of the alleged scheme, with conclusions limited to those supported by the available evidence and accepted forensic practices.

## 1.1 Affected Party
The affected party is Mihir Kishore Jobalia (“the victim”), residing at 19560 Redberry Dr. Los Gatos, CA. 95030. CipherBlade was engaged by the victim’s legal representative, Aylstock, Witkin, Kreis & Overholtz (AWKO), on January 22,2026 to provide investigative and expert services in blockchain forensics. 

## 1.2 Background
This report addresses an alleged impersonation-based cryptocurrency investment fraud resulting in substantial financial loss to the affected party, Mihir Jobalia, a resident of Los Gatos, California. The purpose of this investigation is to document the circumstances surrounding the alleged fraud, establish relevant factual context, and support subsequent blockchain tracing, attribution analysis, and recovery-oriented findings.
The affected party reports that the scheme originated on or about July 22, 2025, when he was contacted via the professional networking platform LinkedIn by an individual using the alias “Elara.” The counterparty represented themselves as a knowledgeable and experienced trader capable of generating outsized returns through cryptocurrency-related investment strategies. At the time of contact, the affected party had no prior experience trading cryptocurrency, a fact materially relevant to the inducement and reliance components of the scheme.
The counterparty promoted an investment opportunity described as spot gold trading conducted through a platform identified as FXCMLABS Global, which was presented as a legitimate trading service. The affected party was instructed to download a mobile application titled FXCMLABS Global from the Apple App Store and to access the associated website, www.fxcrmlabs.com. These representations conveyed the appearance of a regulated or established financial service, contributing to the affected party’s belief that the platform was legitimate.
Between October 2, 2025, and December 2, 2025, the affected party transferred cryptocurrency assets from his account at Kraken to wallet addresses and accounts associated with the FXCMLABS Global platform.  In total the victim conducted 6 transactions that sent a total of 1643.784532 ETH, ≈ $5,195,519.40 to the perpetrator’s fraudulent platform. During this period, the platform displayed account balances and performance indicators reflecting significant unrealized gains, leading the affected party to believe his investment had appreciated by approximately $3.9 million USD. 
The affected party reports that a single withdrawal of $100,000 USD was processed successfully; however, those funds were subsequently redeposited into the platform. Following this redeposit, the affected party was unable to execute further withdrawals. The explanation provided by the counterparty attributed the withdrawal restriction to market volatility, specifically referencing Ethereum price conditions, without requesting additional fees or taxes. Despite repeated attempts, access to the remaining funds was not restored.
On or about December 20, 2025, after continued inability to withdraw assets and growing inconsistencies in platform behavior, the affected party concluded that the investment opportunity was fraudulent. The total reported loss associated with this matter remains $5,090,000 USD, representing the full value of cryptocurrency transferred to the fraudulent platform. Transaction records, wallet identifiers, and supporting documentation were preserved separately and form the evidentiary basis for the analysis contained in this report.
